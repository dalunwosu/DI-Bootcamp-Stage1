BOOKS

The Harry Potter series is a collection of seven fantasy novels written by British author J.K. Rowling. The books follow the life of a young boy, Harry Potter, who discovers on his eleventh birthday that he is a wizard and is whisked away to Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry, a magical school for young witches and wizards. The story is set in a magical version of England and follows Harry as he learns to navigate the wizarding world and comes of age.
The series begins with the first book, Harry Potter and the Philosopher's Stone, in which Harry discovers his true heritage and begins his education at Hogwarts. He quickly makes friends with fellow students Ron Weasley and Hermione Granger, and together they embark on a quest to find the Philosopher's Stone, a powerful magical artifact that is believed to be hidden at Hogwarts. Along the way, they encounter many obstacles, including the villainous Professor Quirrell and the dark wizard Voldemort, who killed Harry's parents and left him with a lightning-shaped scar on his forehead.
As the series progresses, Harry and his friends continue to face off against Voldemort and his followers, known as Death Eaters, in a battle for the fate of the wizarding world. Along the way, they encounter many other challenges, including the return of Voldemort, a secret organization known as Dumbledore's Army, and the revelation of Harry's connection to a prophecy that foretells the defeat of Voldemort.
Throughout the series, Harry, Ron and Hermione become more mature, they have to face the dark reality of the wizarding world, and discover the hidden secrets of their school, the Ministry of Magic, and of their own families. Along the way, they also make many new friends and allies, such as Neville Longbottom, Luna Lovegood, and many others, as well as enemies.
The series ends with the final book, Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows, in which Harry, Ron, and Hermione set out on a journey to destroy the remaining Horcruxes, which are the keys to Voldemort's immortality. They must also confront Voldemort and his followers in a final, epic battle for the fate of the wizarding world. With the help of his friends and allies, Harry ultimately emerges victorious, and Voldemort is defeated once and for all.
The Harry Potter series is renowned for its imaginative world-building, complex characters, and thrilling plot. It has been incredibly popular with both children and adults, and has been translated into numerous languages and adapted into a successful film franchise.

MOVIES

The Harry Potter film series is a collection of eight fantasy films based on the Harry Potter novels written by British author J.K. Rowling. The series chronicles the life of a young orphan boy named Harry Potter, who discovers that he is a wizard and is whisked away to attend Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry.
The first film in the series, "Harry Potter and the Philosopher's Stone" (released as "Sorcerer's Stone" in the United States), was released in 2001. It introduces us to Harry, an orphan living with his cruel aunt and uncle, who learns that he is a wizard and that his parents were murdered by a powerful dark wizard named Lord Voldemort. Harry is accepted into Hogwarts, where he makes new friends, Hermione Granger and Ron Weasley, and learns about magic. Along the way, Harry discovers that Voldemort is still alive and is plotting to return to power.
The second film, "Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets," was released in 2002. In this film, Harry, Ron, and Hermione return to Hogwarts for their second year and uncover a secret chamber within the school that contains a dangerous monster. Along the way, they also learn more about Lord Voldemort's past and the prophecy that states that Harry is the only one who can defeat him.
The third film, "Harry Potter and the Prisoner of Azkaban," was released in 2004. This film sees Harry returning to Hogwarts for his third year, where he discovers that an escaped prisoner named Sirius Black is on the run and believed to be trying to kill him. Along the way, Harry also learns more about his past and the role his family has played in the conflict with Voldemort.
The series continue with Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire, Order of Phoenix, Half-Blood Prince, Deathly Hallows part 1 and 2, which all take place around the life of Harry and friends as they continue to face the challenges of dark wizards and their struggles in the wizarding world and their personal lives. Each film has its own plot, new challenges and mysteries that follow on from the previous films as the story of Harry Potter progresses.
Throughout the series, the films are marked by stunning special effects, an all-star cast, and a faithful adaptation of the source material. With each film the series become darker, it shows the progression of the characters, and the increasing danger they face as they battle against Lord Voldemort. The Harry Potter film series is considered one of the most successful and beloved film franchises of all time, and has gained a huge following of fans around the world.
